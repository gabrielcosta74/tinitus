You are designing "Tinni" — a science-backed tinnitus therapy web app.

WHAT IT IS:
The first tinnitus app that combines clinically proven CBT, personalised sound therapy, 
and AI coaching — and actually measures whether users are getting better. 
750 million people have tinnitus. Most apps are sound machines. Tinni is a real treatment.

WHO IT'S FOR:
People aged 30–65 who have lived with tinnitus for years, tried everything, and feel 
hopeless. They're skeptical of "miracle cures" but desperate for relief. They need to 
trust this is real medicine, not wellness fluff.

THE FEELING THE DESIGN MUST CREATE:
Calm. Hope. Trust. Quiet authority. Like walking into a clinic that actually understands 
you — but built for the modern web. Not "wellness app pastel". Not "cold hospital tech". 
Somewhere in between, leaning warm, trustworthy, scientifically credible.

DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
- Minimalist but warm — not stark
- Spacious typography — large, confident, calm
- Motion is purposeful, not decorative — every animation should reinforce calm
- Trust signals everywhere — data, citations, real numbers
- Mobile-first (60% of users will land on mobile)
- Dark mode is essential (tinnitus is worst at night)

AESTHETIC DIRECTION (you have creative freedom — pick the strongest interpretation):
- Refined editorial: serif headlines, generous whitespace, magazine-like
- Soft organic: subtle gradients, blur effects, calming color washes
- Clean clinical-modern: precise typography, data-forward, swiss-design influence

Pick ONE direction and execute it with confidence. Don't blend three styles.

COLOR INSPIRATION:
Calming teals/greens, warm ambers as accents, off-white/cream backgrounds, 
deep navy or warm charcoal for dark mode. Avoid: purple gradients, generic SaaS blue, 
neon anything, pure black/white.

TYPOGRAPHY DIRECTION:
Pair a distinctive serif for headlines (Fraunces, Söhne, Tiempos, GT Sectra, or similar) 
with a clean modern sans for body (DM Sans, General Sans, Geist, Inter — pick one). 
Generous line-height. Confident type sizes (hero headlines 60–96px desktop).

MOTION GUIDELINES:
- Scroll-triggered reveals (subtle, never bouncy)
- Parallax that feels like breathing, not gimmicky
- A signature animation: a waveform/sound visualisation that calms over time 
  (jagged → smooth) representing the journey
- Number counters that count up on scroll
- Smooth section transitions
- Hover states that are gentle, not jumpy
- Use Framer Motion (or GSAP if you prefer)

WHAT TO AVOID:
- Generic SaaS aesthetic
- Stock photos of people smiling at laptops
- Bright over-saturated colors
- Heavy drop shadows
- Glassmorphism for the sake of it
- Cookie-cutter hero patterns
